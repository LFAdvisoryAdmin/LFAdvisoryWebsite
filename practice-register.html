Tomorrow's email, today

This is the morning summary, grouped by what's overdue, due today, due this week, and what still needs an owner. Copy it into an email to the team. (A later step can automate the send via Power Automate reading the register file.)

Employee leave

Record when staff are away. Any job whose due date falls within a person's leave is flagged red on the list, so you can reassign it or shift the due date.

Add client

Ticking a lodgement generates that client's scheduled jobs for the next 12 months on your target due dates. Re-saving only adds new periods; un-ticking leaves existing jobs in place.

Bulk import jobs

Download the template, fill in one row per job, then upload it back here. CSV or Excel both work.

Client name required
Task required — what needs doing
Due Date required — DD/MM/YYYY or YYYY-MM-DD
Frequency — One-off, Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, Quarterly, Annually, or "Month end + 4" for N business days after month-end (leave Due Date blank for that one)
Responsible — owner's name (optional)
Status — Ready to start, Started, Awaiting info, In review, Completed
Import ATO activity statements

Upload your ATO activity statement listing export (CSV or Excel). Each statement is read by its DIN, named from the form type and period, and only statements not already in the register are added — so you can re-upload each cycle and it brings in just what's new. Monthly statements keep the ATO due date (21st); quarterly are set 3 business days before the agent concession date.

Reads these columns from the export: Client Name, Type (BAS/IAS), Activity Statement Frequency, Name (form type), Period End Date, Due Date, DIN. Owners carry over from existing jobs for the same client.
