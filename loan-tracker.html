Interest Accruals
Daily rate = outstanding principal × annual rate ÷ 365. Days count inclusive of the investment date through to the earlier of month end and loan expiry. Expired loans that have not been finalised keep their principal and accrued interest on the balance sheet until marked finalised.
Historical Blended Rate (WACC)
Month-end blended rate across all loans on foot at each date — finalised loans count up to their expiry, so finalise old loans rather than deleting them to keep the history accurate. Hover for detail; click a point to open that date in the Blended Rate tab.

Weighted by outstanding balance at the selected date. Historical dates include the loans that were on foot then (finalised loans up to their expiry); expired-but-current loans stay included — the money is still out. Future dates are a projection: loans drop off at expiry (rollovers not assumed), scheduled principal repayments are applied, and future-dated loans join when they start — so you can see what expiries and new money do to the rate. Contribution = rate × weight. Daily interest = balance × rate ÷ 365.

Data

Data syncs to SharePoint (LF Advisory Workpapers / lf-advisory-loan-tracker-data.json) via the portal sign-in, with a local copy in this browser as offline fallback. JSON export/import is the backover path for handing the dataset to the client's own environment later.

Conventions
  • All loans are simple interest, daily accrual on an actual/365 basis.
  • End date defaults to start date + term (calendar months) and can be overridden.
  • Interest frequency drives the scheduled payment dates shown in Upcoming. Actual payments are recorded as events and reduce the balance-sheet accrual.
  • Scheduled payment amounts are contractual flat amounts — balance × rate × period/12 (e.g. $6,250/qtr on $166,666.66 @ 15%) — while balance-sheet accruals remain daily actual/365.
  • A capitalisation event adds unpaid accrued interest to principal on the chosen date (the compounding loans) — interest then accrues on the higher balance.
  • A principal repayment event reduces the outstanding balance from the day after the payment date (e.g. half the principal repaid at 6 months).
